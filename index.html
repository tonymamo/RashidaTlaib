About Rashida Tlaib

As the eldest of 14 children and a proud graduate of Detroit Public Schools, I learned early on the importance of hard work, honesty, and commitment to community. Both at the Capitol and in our neighborhoods, my utmost priority remains serving you and your family. This is why I work tirelessly to provide effective and unfaltering advocacy; to amplify the concerns of the families I serve.

To be an effective political leader, legislative advocacy must go hand in hand with direct services. For the past five years, as a State Representative, I've created and run a fully-staffed Service Center—delivering more than ten anti-poverty and quality of life programs throughout my District. As a result, we've helped thousands of families get the resources they so rightly deserve. At its core, our Service Center has been about improving the lives of people locally and across Michigan. Here's a sampling of its many successes:

  • Helped over 400 families save their homes from tax foreclosure.
  • Increased access to weatherization programs to over 250 families.
  • Stopped utility shutoffs for hundreds of families.
  • Gained better city services for thousands of residents.
  • Provided free tax preparation services to over 1,500 families, bringing in over $2 million in refunds that refueled our local economy.
  • Promoted childhood literacy by distributing over 25,000 free books.
  • Connected hundreds of our youth with college admissions representatives and assisted graduating seniors with their financial aid applications.

I was elected to Michigan House of Representatives in 2008. Since then, I've worked on more than 30 bills, combating poverty, increasing access to human services, protecting our health and environment, and promoting public safety and consumer protection. Here's just a sampling of my legislative accomplishments:

  • Obtained over $3 million in education for before/after school programs for our children.
  • Funded the first ever Community Court program, providing alternatives to incarceration for our youth.
  • Required the Michigan Department of Community Health to launch the first ever cancer cluster in our community study to address the public health impact of living close to a polluting industry.
  • Advocated for $1 million for lead abatement programs to protect children from lead poisoning.
  • Passed a bi-partisan bill to combat the crime of mortgage fraud.
  • Created a law requiring consumer protection for residents that fall prey to fraudulent tax preparation schemes.
  • Founded the We Have a Right to Breathe campaign to increase awareness of high rates of asthma, cancer, and pollutants in the air and the need to hold polluting industries accountable.
  • Passed a bill assisting low income families to obtain property tax exemption status.
  • Restored over a $1 million in cuts to our local health clinics.

May it be battling scrap metal thieves, challenging ineffective politicians, or standing up against unfair corporate interests, I continuously put our community first, advocating for our needs and rights. As I make the commitment to run for State Senate, I promise you this:

I will not stop working to make life better for you and our community.

Sincerely,
